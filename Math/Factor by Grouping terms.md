[[Polynomials]] with at least four terms can sometimes be factored by **grouping terms**. The following example illustrates the idea:
$$\begin{align*}
x^{3}+x^{2}+4x4&= (x^3+x^2)(4x+ 4)\\
&= x^2(x+1)+4(x+1)\\
&= (x^{2}+4)(x+1)
\end{align*}$$
$$\begin{align*}
x^{3}-2x^{2}-9x+18&=x^2(x-2)-9(x-2)\\
&= (x^{2}-9)(x-2)\\
&= (x+3)(x-3)(x-2)
\end{align*}$$
